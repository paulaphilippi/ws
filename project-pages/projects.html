READY
In the first study I am leading as part of my PhD, we are aiming to provide a methodologically robust and ecologically valid framework for capturing and modeling developmentally sensitive short-term affective regulation processes following everyday stressful events in the life of youth. To that end, we are using an EMA-design with an innovative high-resolution microburst extension and are following two primary research aims: first, to elucidate the nature of short-term and everyday stress reactions in youth, by examining indices of the stress response and exploring the influence of situational and contextual factors. Second, building upon our findings, we will investigate the influence of person-characteristics such as personality traits and psychopathological symptoms on short-term stress reactivity and recovery.

Preregistration: https://osf.io/tsa4v

STRESSY
I am currently working with Laura Meine, Aleksa Kaurin, and Bj√∂rn Schlier on the project STRESSY, in which the aim is to provide a systematic overview of how stress is operationalized and quantified in the daily lives of youth via EMA designs. This includes descriptive aspects such as item semantics, the variability of daily stress, the frequency/prevalence of assessed stressors, as well as the identification of relationships and covariations between stress indices and mental health outcomes. We intend not only to report on the current state of the literature but also to address emerging challenges of real-time assessments of stress in children and adolescents, ultimately deriving recommendations for future studies.

RehaCAT+
I have worked on the project RehaCAT+ in which we developed a computer-adaptive patient-reported outcome system with integrated feedback systems and action recommendations for therapists.
The project was and is funded by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research and takes place in cooperation with 12 rehabilitation clinics across Germany.
