About Me
Part of my research to date has focused on the effectiveness and acceptance of digital health interventions. More recently, I have increasingly concentrated on improved diagnostics with the goal of early detection and implementation of just-in-time-adaptive interventions for (symptoms of) affective disorders. To that end, I use Ecological Momentary Assessment to study affective dynamics in the daily life of youths and young adults. A methodological focus of my work is to elucidation microtrajectories of negative affect in response to stressful daily life events. I am passionate about the open science movement and complex statistics and analysis methods such as multilevel structural equation models, imputation, and meta-analysis.

Feel free to contact me if you have any questions or would like to chat!
